Publishing
Dr Gregory Lim
Chief Editor, Nature Reviews Cardiology

Gregory Lim studied physiological sciences at the University of Oxford before completing a DPhil in cardiovascular medicine at Merton College, focusing on the role of nitric oxide in cardiac contractility. He joined Nature Reviews Cardiology as an Associate Editor in 2011 and has served as Chief Editor since August 2015.

Dr Francisca Schultz
Senior Subeditor, Nature Magazine

Francisca Schultz is a senior subeditor at Nature, where she edits research articles for clarity, grammar, and style, and coordinates a team of freelance copyeditors. She completed her PhD at the National Heart and Lung Institute with Prof. Julia Gorelik.

Dr Holly Acford-Palmer
Editor (Ecology & Evolution), Nature Communications

Holly Acford-Palmer joined Nature Communications in July 2024 as part of the Ecology & Evolution team, handling submissions in molecular ecology. She previously completed a PhD and postdoctoral research at the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine, using genomics to study malaria transmission, insecticide resistance, and Anopheles mosquito population genetics, and has also worked in clinical microbiome research and diagnostic virology.

Dr Rachel Walters
Senior Scientist, Relation Therapeutics

Rachel Walters completed her PhD at the National Heart and Lung Institute, Imperial College London, studying functional genomics in pulmonary arterial hypertension. After a postdoctoral fellowship in idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis, she is now a Senior Scientist at Relation Therapeutics working on translational genomics and drug discovery.

Dr Megan Barnes
Senior Analyst, FIECON Health Economics

Megan Barnes is a senior analyst at FIECON. She completed her PhD at the National Heart and Lung Institute, where her research focused on respiratory syncytial virus infection in infants using clinical and transcriptomic approaches.

Dr Ravi Jagatia
Associate Clinical Trial Manager, Medpace Oncology

Ravi Jagatia completed a PhD in Cancer Immunology at King's College London, developing immunocompetent 3D ex vivo tumour models for liver cancers. He is currently an Associate Clinical Trial Manager at Medpace, managing oncology clinical trials across early- and late-phase development.

Policy, Law & Entrepreneurship
Dr Tom Wells
Deputy Director, Government Office for Science

Tom Wells completed a PhD in Green Chemistry at Imperial College London before joining the UK Civil Service in 2009. He has held a range of science policy roles spanning research funding, health data, emerging technology and foresight, and is now Deputy Director for Technology Insights at the Government Office for Science.

Dr Samuel Bailey
Associate (Life Sciences), Kilburn Strode

Sam is an associate in Kilburn Strode Life Sciences and Chemistry group, specialising in RNA therapeutics, gene editing and delivery modalities, antibodies, and synthetic biology. He advises on contentious proceedings before the EPO as well as IP strategy, licensing, and global patent portfolio development, and holds a PhD in structural biology.

Dr Aicha El Masri
Partnerships Executive, Imperial Enterprise Division

Aicha El Masri is a Partnerships Executive in the Enterprise Division at Imperial College London, where she develops industry collaborations across the Faculty of Medicine. With a background as a Doctor of Pharmacy (PharmD), she works at the interface of academia and industry to translate research in diagnostics and therapeutics into real-world healthcare impact.

Dr Louie Scott
Commercialisation Executive, Imperial Enterprise Division

Louie Scott is a Commercialisation Executive in the Enterprise Division at Imperial College London, managing a large portfolio of medical intellectual property and supporting licensing and spin-out formation. His expertise lies in MedTech, TechBio, and AI applications in healthcare, and he holds a PhD from the University of Surrey focused on cardiac tissue engineering.

Paths to Academia
Paths to Academia
Dr Alex Ainscough
Assistant Professor, Vascular Bioengineering, NHLI

Dr Alex Ainscough joined the National Heart and Lung Institute (NHLI) at Imperial College London in May 2024 as an Assistant Professor to establish a vascular bioengineering research programme. He previously conducted postdoctoral research with Professor Jennifer Lewis at Harvard University (SEAS and the Wyss Institute). He completed his PhD at Imperial College London, funded by the BHF Centre of Excellence.

Dr Laura Collopy
Senior Teaching Fellow, Genomic Medicine

Dr Laura Collopy is a Senior Teaching Fellow for the MSc Genomic Medicine, leading several core modules and contributing to curriculum design and assessment. She is a Senior Fellow of the Higher Education Academy. Her research background focuses on molecular genetics, telomere biology, rare disorders and cancer genomics, and she holds a PhD in Molecular Genetics.

Dr Gareth Hyde
Director of Operations, NHLI (from Jan 2025)

Dr Gareth Hyde has been Director of Operations at NHLI since 2025. He previously served as Laboratory Manager for the Vascular Science Section, Safety and Technical Services Manager for NHLI, and Head of the Faculty of Medicine Space Programme, overseeing major building projects and space management. He holds a BSc in Biochemistry from the University of Bristol and a PhD in Biomedical Science from the University of Manchester, followed by seven years of postdoctoral research at Manchester and Imperial.

Dr Joana Carrelha
Flow Cytometry Facility Manager

Dr Joana Carrelha is a cell biologist and flow cytometrist, with a BSc and MSc from the University of Lisbon and a DPhil from the University of Oxford. She spent over a decade in Prof. Sten Eirik Jacobsen's laboratory studying haematopoietic stem cells and advanced flow cytometry. She has managed flow cytometry facilities at Imperial and was recently appointed Flow Cytometry Lead at the Ludwig Institute for Cancer Research, Oxford.
